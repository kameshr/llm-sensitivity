IMPLEMENTATION RECOMMENDATIONS
=================================================================

PRIMARY TESTING SEQUENCE:
1. Medical AI (MedQA dataset + custom dosage prompts)
2. Financial Services (FiQA + regulatory compliance)
3. Legal Systems (LegalBench + constitutional law)
4. Emergency Services (custom emergency protocols)

PROMPT DESIGN PRINCIPLES:
- Focus on specific numerical values (dosages, speeds, limits)
- Include procedural sequences (step 1, step 2, etc.)
- Test categorical decisions (safe/unsafe, legal/illegal)
- Cover time-sensitive information (immediate action required)

EVALUATION METRICS BEYOND BERTSCORE:
- Factual Accuracy: Are specific facts correct?
- Safety Violations: Does output recommend unsafe actions?
- Numerical Precision: Are quantities within safe ranges?
- Procedural Correctness: Are steps in proper sequence?

REAL-WORLD IMPACT ASSESSMENT:
- Immediate Danger: Could this output cause immediate harm?
- Long-term Consequences: What are the downstream effects?
- Scope of Impact: How many people could be affected?
- Detectability: Would humans catch this error in practice?

HIGH-PRIORITY SCENARIOS FOR TESTING:
1. Medical dosages (immediate life threat)
2. Financial calculations (economic impact)
3. Emergency procedures (crisis response)
4. Safety thresholds (catastrophic failure)
5. Legal rights (justice system integrity)

RECOMMENDED STARTING POINT:
Begin with MedQA-USMLE + custom medical dosage prompts because:
- Clear right/wrong answers (medical facts are objective)
- Quantifiable harm (easy to assess danger of wrong dosages)
- Real-world relevance (medical AI actively deployed)
- Rich evaluation (multiple types of errors to analyze)
- Public availability (dataset accessible for research)

=================================================================
EXPECTED OUTCOMES
=================================================================

This testing framework should reveal:
1. Which safety-critical domains are most vulnerable
2. Whether certain types of prompts (numerical vs procedural) show higher sensitivity
3. If domain-specific training affects robustness
4. How bit-flip location (sign vs exponent vs mantissa) impacts safety-critical outputs
5. Whether larger models show better or worse robustness in safety scenarios

The goal is to demonstrate that gradient-based sensitivity analysis can identify
critical vulnerabilities in AI systems deployed in high-stakes environments
where model reliability is essential for human safety and welfare.
